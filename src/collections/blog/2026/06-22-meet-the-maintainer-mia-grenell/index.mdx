<div className="intro">
  Continuing in our Meet the Maintainer series, we have{" "}
  <Link to="/community/members/mia-grenell">Mia Grenell</Link>. Mia is a
  maintainer of <a href="https://docs.meshery.io/">Meshery Docs</a>.
  In this interview, we get to know Mia a little better and learn about her
  journey as an open source project maintainer in the Layer5 community.
</div>

<div className="interviewer">
  <span>Bhumika:</span>
  Mia, thank you for taking the time to join me today. While many people, both within and beyond the Layer5 community, have witnessed the impact of your contributions, they may not know the story behind who you are and how you became a maintainer. Could you share your journey with us? How did you discover the Layer5 community, and what inspired you to stay?
</div>

<div className="interviewee">
  <span>Mia:</span>
    "I graduated from the University of Sydney with a strong interest in DevOps, automation, and cloud native technologies, particularly how modern infrastructure management can enable urban data analytics or smart city tech. I discovered the Layer5 community through <Link to="/cloud-native-management/meshery">Meshery</Link> while exploring tools for managing Kubernetes. The project's comprehensive approach to cloud native management immediately stood out. What inspired me to stay was the incredibly welcoming and collaborative community, paired with meaningful opportunities to contribute to documentation and mentorship. The focus on clear, accessible resources and real impact in the ecosystem keeps me engaged."
</div>

<div className="interviewer">
  <span>Bhumika:</span>
  You're a <a href="https://docs.meshery.io/">Meshery Docs</a> maintainer with University of Sydney roots. What does being a <a href="https://docs.meshery.io/">Meshery Docs</a> maintainer mean to you?
</div>

<div className="interviewee">
  <span>Mia:</span>
    "Being a <Link to="/cloud-native-management/meshery">Meshery Maintainer</Link>, tied to my University of Sydney roots, means stewarding high-quality documentation and helping make complex cloud native concepts approachable for everyone. It's about ensuring <a href="https://docs.meshery.io/">Meshery Docs</a> are accurate, comprehensive, and user-friendly, while mentoring contributors. For me, it's a blend of technical precision, community building, and paying forward the support I received when starting out."
</div>

<div className="interviewer">
  <span>Bhumika:</span>
  Have you worked with any other open source project? How does Layer5 compare?
</div>

<div className="interviewee">
  <span>Mia:</span>
    "Yes, I've contributed to or forked projects involving GPU/LLM optimizations and Azure operators. Layer5 stands out for its vibrant, supportive culture, rapid iteration, and strong emphasis on community governance and mentorship programs like <Link to="/programs/gsoc">GSoC</Link> and <Link to="/programs/lfx">LFX</Link>. The energy around <Link to="/cloud-native-management/meshery">Meshery</Link> and the broader ecosystem feels uniquely collaborative and beginner-friendly while tackling advanced cloud native challenges."
</div>

<div className="interviewer">
  <span>Bhumika:</span>
    <Link to="/projects">Layer5 </Link> has a number of active, open source projects. You’ve been consistently contributing to a few of them. Which one(s) are you currently focusing on?
</div>

<div className="interviewee">
  <span>Mia:</span>
    "My primary focus is on <Link to="/cloud-native-management/meshery">Meshery</Link>, particularly the documentation and related educational resources. I also engage with the broader ecosystem through mentorship in programs like <Link to="/programs/gsoc">Google Summer of Code</Link> and <Link to="/programs/lfx">LFX Mentorship</Link>."
</div>

<div className="interviewer">
  <span>Bhumika:</span>
  What's the coolest <a href="https://docs.meshery.io/">Meshery Docs</a> demo you have done/seen?
</div>

<div className="interviewee">
  <span>Mia:</span>
    "One of the most impressive has been live demonstrations showcasing <Link to="/cloud-native-management/meshery">Meshery's</Link> ability to manage multi-cluster Kubernetes environments with visual design patterns and real-time configuration. Seeing complex infrastructure visualized and deployed seamlessly never fails to impress, especially when highlighting <Link to="/cloud-native-management/meshery/integrations">integrations</Link> across clouds and CNCF projects."
</div>

<div className="interviewer">
  <span>Bhumika:</span>
  What is your favorite feature or aspect of the <a href="https://docs.meshery.io/">Meshery Docs</a> in this project, and why?
</div>

<div className="interviewee">
  <span>Mia:</span>
  "My favorite aspect is the strong documentation focus and how it ties into the University of Sydney, bridging academic rigor with practical open source impact. It allows us to create clear pathways for new contributors while advancing real-world DevOps automation."
</div>

<div className="interviewer">
  <span>Bhumika:</span>
  What is your hot tip for working with <Link to="/cloud-native-management/meshery">Meshery</Link> that others may not know?
</div>

<div className="interviewee">
  <span>Mia:</span>
   "Dive into the <Link to="/cloud-native-management/catalog">Meshery Catalog</Link> early and use the visual designer, <Link to="/cloud-native-management/kanvas">Kanvas</Link>, to prototype patterns before writing any YAML. It dramatically speeds up iteration and reduces errors. Also, leverage the performance analysis features. They provide insights that go way beyond basic deployment."
</div>

<div className="interviewer">
  <span>Bhumika:</span>
  Where do you see opportunities for contributors to actively engage and contribute to the <a href="https://docs.meshery.io/">Meshery Docs</a> within the <Link to="/cloud-native-management/meshery">Meshery</Link> and Layer5 community?
</div>

<div className="interviewee">
  <span>Mia:</span>
    "Great entry points include improving documentation, creating tutorials or example patterns, participating in mentorship programs, and contributing to integrations, such as cloud providers or new adapters. The community Slack and weekly meetings are perfect for finding open issues and getting quick feedback."
</div>

<div className="interviewer">
  <span>Bhumika:</span>
  What’s the emoji you use most often? Do you prefer movies or books? Would you consider yourself a morning person or a night owl? Over the past year, what’s a project or accomplishment you’re particularly proud of?
</div>

<div className="interviewee">
  <span>Mia:</span>
    "The emoji I use most often is 🚲 because I'm an avid cyclist. I prefer books; I love diving deep into technical topics and sci-fi. I'm a night owl, engineer by day and biker by night. Over the past year, I'm particularly proud of becoming a <Link to="/cloud-native-management/meshery">Meshery</Link> Maintainer and mentoring contributors through <Link to="/programs/lfx">LFX</Link> and <Link to="/programs/gsoc">GSoC</Link> programs."
</div>

<div className="interviewer">
  <span>Bhumika:</span>
    Do you have any advice for individuals hopeful to become <a href="https://docs.meshery.io/">Meshery Docs</a> contributors or potentially maintainers?
</div>

<div className="interviewee">
  <span>Mia:</span>
    "Start small: pick an open documentation issue, submit a PR, and engage in community discussions. Be consistent, ask questions, and don't hesitate to share your ideas. The community is supportive, and showing initiative with quality contributions is the best path to greater responsibility."
</div>
